Neuroplasticity

25/09/2020

Neuroplasticity is the brains ability to develop neural networks throughout our many stages of life. It was once thought by scientists that the brain was only "plastic" during childhood. While a childs brain can display the greatest plasticity, as adults our brain can also continue to develop neural networks that allow us to learn and grow. Learning new skills, and applyling practice and commitment, can stimulate our brains and encourage neuroplasticity.

I have actively engaged with neuroplasticity throughout my life, namely through learning music and learning how to play a handful of musical instruments. Music can be practiced throughout your entire life, and there are many topics within music that you can explore and improve in. Practice (study) needs to be structured, with an expected outcome. You should ideally practice within a "timebox". This is similar to our EDA learning competencies and the timebox suggestions. Something that is equally as important as practice when it comes to neuroplasticity is sleep! While we sleep our are brains are strengthening the neural pathways we were developing through our day/practice.

Growth Mindset

Adopting a growth mindset will better facilitate neuroplasticity. It is a state of mind where we are willing to accept our failures or shortcomings and have them as a reference for which we can improve on. You cannot grow and continue to learn if you quit after failing once, or if you don't understand something straight away. After experiencing a "fail", we are then able to go back to revise and apply a new, and improved approach to what ever the obstacle was. With a growth mindset, we see ourselves as continuosly improving.
