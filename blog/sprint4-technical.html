Problem Solving

25/09/2020

Each kata this week was challenging, leading us to solve the problems we encountered through many avenues. I often found myself using google to find video tutorials or articles, as well as reading through the JS documentation on MDN or W3Schools. Check out devdocs.io, a cool documentation solution.

During the JS Olympics, we had to unjumble a word using string and array methods built into JS. I began to read the documentation to find the methods and learn how to utilise them. I used .split(), .reverse(), and .join() and began to construct my solution. When I passed the test, I looked at how convoluted my solution was. I then looked at the sample solution that was provided and laughed at how simple the solution could be. I learned that I could chain these methods together on a single line. The image below shows my initial solution which is commented out, and the single line solution that was provided.



There are many problem solving techniques we can utilise. I often begin with using google, with a search query like "average an array javascript". Sometimes I will get an easy answer, and I can implement a solution in my code. Other times I will need to dig deeper, and that usually leads me to reading the JS documentation. I will often need to watch a tutorial on what it was I was reading about in the documentation and from that video I will be able to see examples, as well as catch a couple tips.

When I encounter a problem that I feel capable of solving myself with the knowledge I have, I will begin to use the programming environment to support me. This means I'll refer back to and refine my pseudocode. I'll also read error messages and start to console.log() to get a better look at what my function or object/array is returning. Speaking aloud or "rubber ducky" also works for me, and it is happening throughout the learning process in general. Sometimes I will message a friend who knows programming, and this proves to be productive, as I am typing and sharing the problem and while I might not get the specific solution, I gain further insight.
