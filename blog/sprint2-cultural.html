Learning Plan

Sprint 2 Cultural Blog

12/09/2020

After a couple of months learning web development at home I decided to enrol at EDA. I was making good progress learning at home, but I really wanted to be amongst people that were also working towards a common goal. I want to work as a web developer/programmer. I am not yet sure which kind specifically (front-end/back-end development - or something programming adjacent!?), but I hope to discover where my strengths in this field are in the coming weeks.

I wanted to follow a more structured and result oriented curriculum. I learned that when you work in the web development/programming/tech industry you are often working in a team. I want to gain the skills necessary to thrive in these environments and I want to be an excellent team player. While I have proven success working in a team in my previous jobs, I am happy to refine these skills and apply them to a new industry.

I have direct communication to my foundations facilitators, as well as all the students in our EDA Slack channel. I have already really enjoyed this Slack channel.

Something I did not have a great grasp on when learning at home before EDA was time management. Since sprint 1 I have been using the timebox suggestions as well as the pomodoro timing technique. I really enjoy using pomodoro as I have experienced the efficiency for myself.

In my life and learning, I often seek help and advice from my family and peers. When it comes to learning new skills, I am quick to research and find solutions to obstacles that I come across. This is a default of mine as I am passionate about learning and growing.
