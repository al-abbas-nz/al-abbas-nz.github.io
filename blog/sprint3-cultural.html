Emotional Intelligence

EQ

19/09/2020

Emotional intelligence, also known as emotional quotient (EQ), is our ability to understand and manage our emotions, and relating that experience to others. It is different to intelligence quotient (IQ), as IQ is determined through standardized tests and measures intellectual abilities that pertain to memory, visual processing, knowledge, and quantitative reasoning (e.g. math skills).

EQ is something we can develop, and it proves to be beneficial in our lives. Improving our social and emotional skills can help in our personal and professional lives. By being concious of the emotions of others, we can begin to further develop our empathy. We can bring out the best in ourselves and others. Some ways we can practice our EQ include: reflecting on our emotions and learning to control them, evaluating how others feel, being an active and engaged listener, and being aware and observing the body language of others and ourselves.
