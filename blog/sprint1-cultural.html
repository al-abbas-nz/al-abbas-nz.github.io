Identity, Values and Strengths.

Sprint 1 Cultural Blog

12/09/2020

I’ve been thinking about how our experiences can influence our values and identity, and vice versa. I would consider adventure, creativity, and growth to be a few of my core values.

Adventure is a value of mine because taking leaps into the unknown has provided me with great experiences. My greatest example would be when I moved to NYC to work as a bike messenger, after becoming obsessed with cycling earlier that year. I’m diving into code now and I look forward to whatever this brings.

I value creativity because I see it as something we can develop and grow through many outlets. From art, cooking, to problem solving. To me this also ties in with being a lifelong learner. I have found opportunities and communities through creative pursuits such as music.

And growth, I reckon I am at EDA now because of aligning with the aforementioned values. My last job in the music industry (record shop) could not accommodate for future growth so I was in a fortunate position to resign.

My brief time as a bike messenger provided me with a lot of growth. I gained a lot of strength physically and mentally as it is a demanding job. Even before that experience I had to push myself through challenging times, and I feel that I have a lot of tenacity to push through tough moments that may not last forever. I apply this persistence and drive to my daily activities, and especially when it comes to learning new skills.

The education I am pursuing now with the help of EDA I feel is crucial. At my previous job I often had ideas that I simply could no execute because I lacked the technical ability. I would often share these ideas but not being able to bridge the gap from idea to reality was frustrating. I am looking forward to minimizing my limitations by pursuing this education.
